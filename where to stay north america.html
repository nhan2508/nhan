1. Solage Calistoga, Napa Valley, California, USA


As the ground thaws and flowers blossom, there’s never been a better time take advantage of North America’s natural beauty. Springtime is the perfect opportunity to take time out and enjoy the outdoors, and one of the best times to explore or take a romantic break. Get some inspiration for where to go this season from our top 10 places to stay in North America this Spring. Most people head for Napa Valley during the Summer of harvest season, but Spring is just as glorious, without the crowds. With temperatures in the high 60s and 70s, this is a very pleasant time to head for Solage Calistoga with its full range of spa services, unique wellness experiences, a Michelin-starred restaurant and complimentary cruiser bikes to explore the resort and the surrounding area. Spa Solage was voted #1 in the Americas and the Caribbean by Conde Nast Traveller, and the Michelin Star rated Solbar, named “Best New Restaurant” in Napa Valley by Napa/Sonoma Magazine. Here you’ll find a focus is on offering a healthy, thoughtful balance. Gracious and green. Indulgent and nourishing. Sophisticated and whimsical. Solage takes the natural and makes it spectacular with organic design, conscientious service and attention to detail. Get away to a brand of style that’s naturally brilliant…



2. The Inn at Bowman’s Hill, New Hope, Pennsylvania, USA


Less than 90 minutes’ drive from Manhattan, in the heart of Bucks County, Pennsylvania, lies the riverside town of New Hope. A Springtime visit to this vibrant tourist destination on the banks of the scenic Delaware River is an energizing experience. Bike among the daffodils along the Delaware canal towpath, take a horse-drawn carriage ride, dine overlooking the river or sing-along at a piano bar – this is a world apart from big city life. New Hope has a rich historic and cultural heritage that is today alive and well. Equity theater, cabaret, live music venues, a working railroad, art museums, antiques, nature preserves, traditional taverns, award-winning restaurants – and six wineries within 5 miles. New Hope is also home to The Inn at Bowman’s Hill, one of the most romantic inns in the nation. Nestled on a 5-acre, gated estate surrounded by pond and forest, the inn is a 10-year recipient of the prestigious AAA Four-Diamond Award. It features four luxurious guest rooms and four suites, including the 800 square-foot Penthouse. King-size featherbeds, fireplaces, steam showers, in-suite massage services, and gourmet, cooked-to- order breakfast choices are the norm. Blossoms, manicured grounds, a heated pool, orchid conservatory, and just across the road, the 130-acre, Bowman’s Hill Wildflower Preserve, complete the picture and make this beautiful Bucks County inn a perfect spring getaway.


3. Chilko Experience, British Columbia, Canada


Hidden gem is a term so overused and misapplied by the travel industry it has lost its significance. However, in the case of the Chilko Experience it is true. A seven home $10M private estate located on a pristine 5M acre wilderness tract in southern British Columbia it has recently opened to the public. Architecturally stunning it has been featured on HGTV Canada. Ecologically significant, it is overwhelms the senses.Spring is a special time at Chilko. Billions of Aspens regain their leaves, mountains show their peaks, rivers rise, rainbow trout hunger, bears awaken, new born fawns wobble and vistas sparkle under the clear skies. To be truly appreciated nature it must be embraced, closely. Kayak or cruise the 50 mile long Chilko Lake, hike the mountain trails, fish the lakes, saddle up and ride to mountain top fossil beds or ATV 500 miles of backwoods trails. Spring is all about renewal and rebirth. Chilko is a wonderful place to participate in it.
